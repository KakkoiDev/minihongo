# The Minihongo Dilemma: Minimal vs Practical

Two products are fighting for one URL.

---

## The Tension

| Product A: "The Experiment" | Product B: "The Tool" |
|---|---|
| 206 words, say anything | Actually navigate life in Japan |
| Linguistic minimalism | Practical survival |
| "Look how few words you need" | "Here's what to say at the doctor" |
| Audience: curious learners, linguists | Audience: refugees, NGO volunteers |
| Wow factor: the constraint | Wow factor: it actually works |
| Ships fast, stays small | Grows with every real need |

The original vision: 206 words + minimal grammar prove you can express any idea in Japanese. Beautiful. Clean. A linguistic experiment backed by NSM, Swadesh, Toki Pona research.

The real world: someone needs to say "station," order food without allergens, register their address at city hall. The 206 words can't handle that without awkward circumlocutions. So we add loan words, daily-life dialogs, convenience store scripts. Each addition is justified. Each one dilutes the original promise.

---

## What Happened

The project started minimal and drifted practical:

- **206 base words** - the core. Solid.
- **39 grammar points** - reorganized around 3 patterns. Clearer than before.
- **202 kanji compounds** - built from the 206. On-brand.
- **938 expressions** - circumlocutions. The "infinite expression" proof. On-brand.
- **69 loan words** - katakana words for daily life. Useful but outside the 206.
- **26 dialogs** - 12 new daily-life situations (doctor, pharmacy, train station, city hall). Practical but requires vocabulary outside the 206 (e.g. "station" is not a base word).
- **7 stories** - literary. On-brand.
- **32 haiku** - literary. On-brand.

The bloat isn't the volume. It's the lack of a clear boundary between "the 206 experiment" and "everything else."

---

## The Problem with Feedback

No NGO/volunteer feedback yet. Two risks:

1. **Ship too minimal** - NGOs say "this is interesting but my students need to buy train tickets." The experiment is admired but not adopted.

2. **Ship too bloated** - NGOs say "this is overwhelming, I thought it was 206 words." The simplicity promise breaks on first contact.

The worst outcome: spending months expanding content that nobody asked for, when the core 206 + 3 patterns might be exactly what's needed.

---

## Three Options

### Option 1: Stay Minimal

Kill loan words. Kill the new dialogs. Keep the original 5 daily dialogs and 7 stories. Accept that 206 words won't get you through a doctor visit. The value is the insight, not the utility.

**Ship:** 206 words + grammar + original reading content.
**Message:** "Learn the system. The words will come."
**Risk:** Admired but not adopted. Too academic for real use.

### Option 2: Stay Practical

Accept that 206 is a starting point, not the ceiling. Ship everything. Add more dialogs as needs emerge. The value is that it works in real situations.

**Ship:** Everything we built.
**Message:** "Start with 206, grow from there."
**Risk:** Loses the "206 words" identity. Becomes another Japanese learning resource. Nothing differentiates it.

### Option 3: Layer It

One URL, two experiences. Clear boundary between core and extensions.

**Core layer (the 206 experiment):**
- Vocabulary: 206 words
- Grammar: 3 patterns + all grammar points (core-flagged ones highlighted)
- Reading: stories, haiku, original dialogs
- PDF: this layer only

**Extension layer (the practical tool):**
- Word-building: compounds, expressions, loan words
- Daily-life dialogs: doctor, station, restaurant, city hall
- Clearly marked "Beyond 206" everywhere
- Website only (not in PDF)

**Ship:** Both layers, clearly separated.
**Message:** "206 words, infinite expression. And when you need more, it's here."
**Risk:** Complexity of maintaining the boundary. But the boundary already exists (core badges, "not part of 206" framing, PDF without word-building).

---

## What Would Unblock Feedback

The fastest path to real feedback:

1. **Ship the PDF to 2-3 NGOs.** The PDF is already minimal (vocab + grammar + reading). It's self-contained. A volunteer can print it and use it in a classroom tomorrow.

2. **Ask two questions:**
   - "Can your students make sentences with this?"
   - "What situation came up that this didn't cover?"

3. **Let the answers decide.** If they say "we need doctor dialogs" - build Product B. If they say "this is exactly right" - stay with Product A.

Building more content before feedback is building in the dark.

## Resolution (2026-03-30)

The dilemma was a framing error. The 12 daily-life dialogs were already written using 206-word circumlocutions. They were never "beyond 206" - they were the strongest proof that the system works.

The few remaining leaks (えき, コンビニ, カード, abbreviated day names, 番) were replaced with pure 206-word alternatives:

| Concept | Was | Now (206 words) |
|---|---|---|
| Station | えき | 人が来る物を待つ所 |
| Convenience store | コンビニ | 買う所 |
| Card (payment) | カード | お金の小さい物 |
| Monday | げつの日 | 月の日 |
| Wednesday | すいの日 | 水の日 |
| Friday | きんの日 | お金の日 |
| Platform number | 番 | つ目 (ordinal) |
| Welcome greeting | いらっしゃいませ | 来てくれてありがとう |

The daily-life dialogs moved from Extension to Core. The 206-word claim is now fully intact across all content.

Kanji compounds and loan words archived to ARCHIVE-LOANWORDS-COMPOUNDS.md for reference.
